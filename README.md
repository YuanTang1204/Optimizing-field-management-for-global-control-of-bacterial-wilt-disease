# Optimizing-field-management-for-global-control-of-bacterial-wilt-disease
## Model card-Global bacterial wilt incidence prediction for tomato and potato croplands
### Model details
a)	The global crop bacterial wilt incidence regressor provided by this work is trained to estimate the bacterial wilt disease incidence(DI, %) across major crops such as tomato and potato, based on multiple influencing factors, including climate variables, soil physicochemical properties, fertilization type and frequency, irrigation, and tillage practices.
b)	Developed by researchers at NWAFU in 2025.
### Intended Use
a)	Intended to be used for promoting sustainable crop disease management, such as optimizing fertilizer and tillage practices to mitigate bacterial wilt outbreaks.
b)	Intended to be used for further research in agricultural sustainability, soil -plant microbe interactions, and environmental data science.
c)	Not intended to estimate the incidence of other plant diseases beyond bacterial wilt in tomato and potato.
### Factors
Relevant factors include climatic conditions, soil physicochemical properties, fertilization and tillage practices, irrigation amount, and crop types.
### Metrics
The coefficient of determination (R²) and root mean square error (RMSE) are reported to evaluate the consistency between observed and predicted bacterial wilt disease incidence (DI).
### Ethical Considerations
The model supports the sustainable reduction of bacterial wilt risks without increasing chemical input intensity, offering scientific evidence for AI-assisted, low-impact agricultural management strategies.
### Training Data
80% of the collected dataset was used for model training.
### Test Data
20% of the collected dataset was used for independent model test.
### Caveats and Recommendations
Due to data limitations, some potentially relevant factors, such as soil microbial community composition, pathogen genetic diversity, and real-time precipitation timing, were not included. Future research should incorporate these aspects for improved prediction accuracy.
